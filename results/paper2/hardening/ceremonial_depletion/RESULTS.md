## Conclusion
Ceremonial sites are overall mildly enriched on the corridor, not depleted. The temporal decomposition reveals a striking gradient: pre-2000 BCE ceremonial sites are **very strongly enriched** (Z=+7.6), consistent with the mortuary signal. The enrichment weakens through later periods as Mediterranean Classical temples and post-Roman churches proliferate in regions the circle avoids. The Phase 5 decomposition confirms churches (Z=-1.8) drive the late-period depletion, while temples/sanctuaries remain enriched overall (Z=+3.8). Combined sacred sites (mortuary + ceremonial) pre-2000 BCE show extraordinary enrichment (Z=+32.3), far exceeding settlement enrichment (Z=+6.4), confirming a strong sacred-architecture corridor signal in the pre-2000 BCE period.
