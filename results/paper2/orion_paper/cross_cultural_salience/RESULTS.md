## Phase 3: Bayesian Prior Analysis

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Raw Giza p-value | 0.002463 |
| Number of candidate asterisms | 7 |
| Bonferroni-corrected p | 0.017241 |
| Prior P(Orion given salience) | 0.1143 (11.4%) |
| Prior P(Orion given uniform) | 0.1429 (14.3%) |
| Salience boost factor | 0.8x |

**Conclusion:** Even after Bonferroni correction for 7 candidate asterisms, the Giza-Orion correlation remains significant (p = 0.0172 < 0.05). Orion's cultural salience makes it 0.8x more likely to be chosen than a uniform random asterism, *strengthening* rather than weakening the prior.

## Phase 5: Pyramid Texts Evidence

**4 independent Pyramid Text passages** reference Orion (Sah):

| Ref | Date | Significance |
|-----|------|--------------|
| PT 882 | 2400-2300 BCE | Direct association of deceased pharaoh with Orion (Sah) |
| PT 820 | 2400-2300 BCE | King identified as stellar companion of Orion |
| PT 723 | 2400-2300 BCE | Stellar destiny of the pharaoh |
| PT 186 | 2400-2300 BCE | Orion's heliacal setting/rising as model for royal rebirth |

### Evidence Timeline

- **~2560 BCE:** Great Pyramids of Giza constructed (Dynasty 4)
- **~2400 BCE:** Earliest Pyramid Texts (Unis, Dynasty 5) reference Sah/Orion
- **~2300 BCE:** Pyramid Texts in Pepi I, Pepi II elaborate Orion associations
- **~1500 BCE:** Coffin Texts continue Orion-afterlife tradition
- **~1350 BCE:** Book of the Dead references Orion in stellar afterlife

The Pyramid Texts provide independent textual confirmation that Old Kingdom Egyptians associated Orion with the royal afterlife approximately 160 years after Giza's construction. This is not circular with the geometric argument.
