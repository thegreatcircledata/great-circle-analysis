# Ambiguous Claims Resolution — Summary

**Date**: 2026-03-21

## Claim 1: 43,200 Scale Factor → **MIXED**

**What's real**: The Great Pyramid's proportions DO approximate a 1:N scale model of Earth's
northern hemisphere. The dual match (one multiplier connecting perimeter→circumference AND
height→radius) occurs in only **3.0%** of random buildings. The geometric relationship is genuine.

**What's not**: The specific number 43,200 is NOT optimal. The best multiplier for circumference
is **43,492**; for radius, **43,364**. The number 43,200 (= 600 × 72) appears chosen for sexagesimal
elegance. Overall hit count (numerological matches) is NOT special — the GP sits at the 23rd
percentile among random monuments (z = −0.62).

**Bottom line**: The pyramid encodes Earth's shape. The number 43,200 is a post-hoc round number,
not a precision constant.

---

## Claim 2: Orion Intentionality → **PARTIALLY SUPPORTED**

**What's real**: The Giza layout matches Orion's Belt significantly better than random plateau
arrangements (p = 0.0015 unconstrained, p = 0.0074 collinearity-matched). Orion ranks **#5 out
of 3,420** bright-star triplets tested (top 0.15%). The shape match is genuine and Orion is among
the very best astronomical correspondences.

**What's not**: 4 other star triplets match BETTER than Orion (Arcturus-Regulus-Alhena, etc.),
though these are not culturally significant constellations. More critically, the match quality
varies by only **0.000088** across 18,000 years — less than 1% of survey measurement uncertainty
(±0.0094). ALL epochs produce the same match. The 10,500 BCE date claim has **zero geometric support**.

**Bottom line**: The shape correspondence is real and statistically significant. The date claim
is meaningless — the match is epoch-invariant.

---

## Claim 3: Pillar 43 Stellar Encoding → **NOT SUPPORTED**

**What's real**: Nothing in this analysis supports the claim.

**What's not**:
- The claimed animal→constellation mapping ranks **#22 out of 24** possible permutations (near worst)
- Random arrangements match BETTER than the actual carvings (p = 0.99 — the actual arrangement is WORSE than chance)
- The total variation across epochs is **0.03%** — all dates are equally bad
- Among 6,840 possible constellation assignments, the claimed set ranks **#689** (10th percentile)
- The best-matching constellation set is Sagittarius-Cygnus-Aquila, not the claimed Sagittarius-Scorpius-Libra

**Bottom line**: The Sweatman & Tsikritsis hypothesis fails all four tests. The claimed stellar
encoding does not exist in the data.

**Caveat**: Carving positions are approximate (from published figures). However, the random
arrangement test controls for this — and the actual carvings perform WORSE than random,
meaning more precise positions would not rescue the hypothesis.
