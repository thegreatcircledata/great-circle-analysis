# Cross-Site Ancient Construction Precision Survey — Research Proposal

**Prepared:** 2026-03-22
**Context:** Phase 2B output of the Construction Anomaly Index project

---

## The Problem

Despite decades of popular and academic fascination with ancient construction precision, **no systematic cross-site measurement database exists.** The most-cited precision measurements in the field are Flinders Petrie's 1883 caliper readings at Giza — never independently verified with modern instruments. Of 12 major ancient sites commonly cited for exceptional stonework precision, 7 have zero published numerical tolerance measurements (joint gap, surface flatness, or angular accuracy). Three more have only qualitative descriptions ("fractions of a millimeter," "a knife blade cannot fit").

This gap makes cross-cultural comparison of construction capability impossible, leaves popular claims about "anomalous" precision unverifiable, and prevents any rigorous test of whether construction precision correlates with geographic, temporal, or cultural patterns.

## Current Data Landscape

### Tier 1: Quantified (numerical precision data published)
- **Great Pyramid of Giza** — Petrie 1883: 0.5mm joint gaps, 0.13mm/m surface flatness, 12 arcsec angular accuracy. Dash 2016: 46mm side variation over 230m. The richest precision dataset for any ancient site worldwide.

### Tier 2: Partial / Contested
- **Serapeum of Saqqara** — Antropogenez 2021: ~1.1 angular deviation (contradicts Dunn's micrometer claims). Digital angle gauge only.
- **Puma Punku** — Protzen & Nair 2013: ±1mm stated tolerance. Ruler and caliper measurement.

### Tier 3: Qualitative Only
- **Sacsayhuaman** — "fractions of a millimeter" (Protzen 1985). No calibrated gap measurement.
- **Göbekli Tepe** — Pillar placement geometry only (Haklay & Gopher 2020). No surface measurements.
- **Puma Punku platform slabs** — Mass and transport data only.

### Tier 4: Unmeasured
- Ollantaytambo Six Monoliths
- Baalbek Trilithon
- Stonehenge sarsens (sub-mm scan exists but restricted; joint gaps never extracted)
- Malta (Hagar Qim, Mnajdra, Ggantija)
- Easter Island (Ahu Vinapu)
- Osireion at Abydos
- Valley Temple at Giza

## Proposed Survey

### Objective
Establish the first standardized cross-site database of ancient construction precision measurements, enabling quantitative comparison of stonework tolerances across cultures, periods, and geographies.

### Measurement Protocol
For each site, measure the following using calibrated instruments:

| Measurement | Instrument | Minimum Spec | Sampling |
|------------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Joint gap width | Feeler gauges (0.01mm) or laser gap sensor | ±0.05mm resolution | 5 joints per wall/course, 3 readings per joint |
| Surface flatness | Precision straight-edge (1m) + dial indicator; or structured light scanner | ±0.1mm over 1m | 3 surfaces per block, 5 blocks per site |
| Angular accuracy | Digital angle gauge | ±0.05° | All accessible corners |
| Surface roughness Ra | Portable profilometer | ±1µm | 3 readings per dressed surface |

### Minimum Viable Dataset
- **6 sites** with full measurement protocol (all 4 measurement types)
- **3 sites** must be non-Egyptian to enable cross-cultural comparison
- **1 control site** with known construction method and period (e.g., Roman quarry face, medieval cathedral)
- **Total:** ~150 individual measurements across 7+ sites

## What This Would Settle

1. **Is the Great Pyramid uniquely precise, or is Giza just the only place anyone measured?** Currently unanswerable.
2. **Does Sacsayhuaman really have "fractions of a millimeter" joint gaps?** 40 years of citation without verification.
3. **Do sites near the Alison Great Circle share construction precision characteristics?** Requires measurements at Ollantaytambo, Valley Temple, Sacsayhuaman, Easter Island — all currently at zero data.
4. **Is the Serapeum dispute resolvable?** Yes, with a single CMM session.
5. **Does construction precision peak in the 3rd millennium BCE and decline thereafter?** Requires temporal coverage beyond just Giza.

## What This Would NOT Settle

- Whether any specific construction method was used (precision alone doesn't determine technique)
- Whether "lost technology" existed (anomalous precision relative to known tools does not prove unknown tools)
- Whether cross-site precision similarity implies contact or common ancestry (could be convergent)

The goal is to replace adjectives with numbers.
